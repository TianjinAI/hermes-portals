## 1. Overview: What is MacroMicro?

**MacroMicro** (macromicro.me) is a macro research platform that produces data-driven analysis at the intersection of geopolitics, monetary policy, inflation, and technology cycles. Their content is characterized by:

- **Deep data integration:** Every argument is backed by proprietary charts, dashboards, and indices hosted on their platform.
- **Multi-asset coverage:** Equities, FX, bonds, commodities, central bank policy, and international political economy.
- **Signature Products:**
  - **WEFC (Weekly Economic and Financial Commentary):** A weekly report with a 20+ page chart pack covering the most critical market-moving events.
  - **CEO House View:** Monthly strategy piece from CEO Rachel Chen setting macro allocation themes.
  - **Deep-dive reports:** Thematic research on AI supply chains, US-China tech competition, semiconductor cycles.
  - **Flash analysis:** Rapid-response pieces on geopolitical events (notably the US-Iran conflict).
- **Style:** Analytical, data-heavy, forward-looking. Combines top-down macro with bottom-up supply chain analysis. Strong emphasis on AI as a structural macro force.
- **Authors:** Rachel Chen (CEO), Ralice, Jat, Jordan (editor), and the broader MM research team.

### #2 — April 9, 2026 | 🎇 Is Inflation Spiraling? Oil, Tariffs & AI in the New Pricing Cycle

**Type:** Thematic deep-dive (Author: Ralice, Editor: Jordan)

**Key Topics:**

1. **Inflation Was Building Before the Conflict:** January/February PPI exceeded expectations for two consecutive months (YoY >3%). ISM Manufacturing Prices subcomponent surged to 70.5 in Feb → 78.3 in March — highest since July 2022.

2. **Ceasefire Drama:** Hours before Trump's April 7 deadline, a dramatic reversal: two-week temporary ceasefire announced. US-Iran representatives to meet in Islamabad on April 10. Brent and WTI plummeted from >$110 to ~$95 on the news.

3. **Three Inflation Drivers Identified:**
   - **Oil/Energy Shock:** Short-term CPI push. Eurozone March inflation already jumped to 2.5% (from 1.9%). US March CPI estimated ~3.5% YoY.
   - **Tariffs:** Core Goods CPI climbed from <0% to 1.5% YoY. Steel effective tariff rate +25pp, aluminum +37pp. Steel Mill Products PPI +22%, Aluminum Mill Shapes +41% since end-2024.
   - **AI-Driven Demand:** Electronic Components & Accessories PPI surged to ~18% YoY. Computer Software & Accessories CPI maintained >5% YoY (vs. historical sub-zero). AI data center buildout driving metals demand (aluminum for racks, copper for cabling).

4. **Why Runaway Inflation Is Unlikely:**
   - **Energy shock contained:** US is now 20% of global crude production (vs. OPEC >50% in 1970s). Energy as share of PCE fell from ~40% to 24%. Energy intensity of GDP down ~40% since 2000.
   - **Shelter disinflation anchor:** Rent of Primary Residence and Owners' Equivalent Rent projected to decelerate through 2026 (Zillow data). Shelter = 40%+ of CPI weight.
   - **AI productivity = disinflationary:** Benign price hikes drive industry upgrades → productivity gains → eventually suppress prices. Analogous to 1990s Internet productivity boom.
   - **Limited CPI weight:** Electricity (2.5%) + IT Hardware/Services (1.7%) = even 20% surge would add <1% to headline CPI.

5. **Forward Catalyst:** Kevin Warsh taking over as Fed Chair after mid-May. Trump-Xi summit. If market reaction to negative data dulls → upward trend anchored by productivity.

**Charts/data referenced:** PPI items YoY, ISM Prices, Cleveland Fed CPI nowcast, Brent/WTI futures, US crude production share, energy expenditure share of PCE, Sticky Price CPI, Zillow rent forecasts, Computer/Electronic component PPI, CPI weighting breakdown.
